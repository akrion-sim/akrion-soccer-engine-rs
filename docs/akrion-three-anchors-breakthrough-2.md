The core finding: parity isn't a score you're losing, it's a ceiling you built
Three independent mechanisms in the current architecture each cap the neural policy at "reproduce the analytic engine." Any one of them alone is sufficient to explain the plateau. That's why single levers keep failing — fixing one leaves the other two binding. The ~9 falsified levers were almost all in the wrong subspace: they tuned which of analytic's options gets picked, while the three anchors that actually pin you to analytic went untouched.

Anchor 1 — the policy re-ranks analytic's own candidate set
Decisions are neural-authoritative: Q_eff = λ·V_net + 1e-6·Q_tab (soccer.rs value-collapse), but the candidates being ranked are generated and spatially executed by analytic. MCTS that could search past that is off by default, depth 1 (soccer.rs:18533-18567) — the "simulation" is a visit-count re-rank of ~4 precomputed candidates, no rollout. MPC is a feasibility filter, not a planner. → The net can reorder analytic's options; it cannot propose plays analytic wouldn't. Ceiling = analytic's candidate generation.

Anchor 2 — the critic is trained to imitate an aliased tabular value
The neural value target bootstraps off the tabular Q: target = r + γ·best_value_hierarchical(s') (world.rs:18178-18205). The tabular Q aliases wildly different 22-player states onto one bin. So the critic regresses onto an imitation of analytic and structurally cannot exceed it — this is exactly what dp-critic-target-plan.md diagnoses, and why MC-critic and self-bootstrap both falsified. (On top of this, the value head had collapsed to a constant from low-variance targets — fixed by DD_SOCCER_ENABLE_TARGET_STANDARDIZATION, but that's necessary-not-sufficient: it still lands at 0.478.)

Anchor 3 — the only dense long-horizon reward is a potential over a hand-built xT that nets to zero
The territorial/off-ball reward is proper telescoping PBRS — F = γ·Φ(s') − Φ(s) (reward_shaping.rs:40) — where Φ = expected_threat = fwd³ + 0.8·fwd⁴·centrality, a closed-form analytic seed (pitch_value.rs:144). Because it's a proper potential, over any possession that returns to its starting field position it sums to exactly zero. So the only non-cancelling long-horizon signal is the sparse goal (±160, ~1/game) — and Anchor 2 can't propagate it. This is "territory without conversion," by construction. It's why every territory/reward-shaping arm was policy-invariant or flat.

And the objective never even aims at the target
The decisive independent finding: training never plays the analytic engine. The league optimizes "beat frozen neural past-selves + fresh random nets" (soccer_league_train.rs:1149); analytic appears only at eval (SOCCER_EVAL_ANALYTIC_FIELD). The one flag that puts analytic in training — SOCCER_LEAGUE_ANALYTIC_OPPONENTS=1 (:1163) — is set nowhere. The 0.53-vs-field / 0.35-vs-analytic split is the textbook fingerprint of a train/eval objective mismatch. You cannot climb a metric you never optimize.

The breakthrough: cut the three anchors together, in this order
The good news buried in your own repo: most of this is built but unwired/untested in the right combination, not net-new research.

Move 0 — Train against analytic (free, do first, high information). Set SOCCER_LEAGUE_ANALYTIC_OPPONENTS=1 (keep some self-play for diversity) and build the missing asymmetric neural-vs-analytic eval ladder. One-line change, no rebuild risk. This directly tests whether the objective mismatch is first-order, and exposes the critic to the states analytic's style (≈2× your passing volume) drives you into. If the architecture can't represent better, you'll find out in hours instead of weeks.

Move 1 — Replace the imitation anchors with outcome-grounded value (the core). Two wiring changes, both using machinery that already exists:

1a. Swap expected_threat → learned_epv inside the potential (team_expected_threat/territorial_advantage/soccer_replay_pitch_value_potential, pitch_value.rs:353/418). The fitted-EPV grid already exists end-to-end (fit_epv_grid.py: goal=1.0/SOT=0.30/turnover=−0.15, γ-discounted) but is currently wired only into a per-pass bonus (world.rs:22879, gate DD_SOCCER_ENABLE_LEARNED_EPV, off). Putting it in the potential makes advancing into a cell that historically converts worth more than sterile territory — directly attacking Anchor 3.
1b. Break the tabular-Q bootstrap: use the DP-value-iterated V_DP(bucket) as the critic anchor instead of best_value_hierarchical (DD_SOCCER_ENABLE_DP_CRITIC_TARGET, dp-critic-target-plan.md) — with target-standardization on so it doesn't re-collapse. Coarse outcome-grounded DP backbone + neural residual is the theoretically-clean value function (adp-can-break-the-ceiling.md Tier 3) and is the genuinely-untried combination.
Move 2 — Give the value something worth resolving: a relational encoder. To exceed analytic's hand-built xT, the critic must read 22-player geometry better than a hand-crafted 610-dim readout can. Your offline-distilled head prototype already showed ~47% held-out gain but isn't in live play. Distill a set-transformer/GNN encoder within the 66ms budget (DD_SOCCER_ENABLE_OFFLINE_DISTILLED_HEAD). Without this, outcome-grounded targets can't be fit precisely enough to beat analytic.

Move 3 — Convert (the bottleneck Moves 0-1 will expose). Deferred-pass-credit already proved you get forward but don't finish. Two real interface gaps (unlike the redundant forward-pass action): (a) Shoot carries only power — placement is chosen analytically; give the actor placement. (b) Turn on real shallow search with the now-outcome-grounded value as the MCTS leaf (depth>1) so the policy can plan a conversion sequence instead of reacting. This is the AlphaZero move — but it only works after Moves 1-2, which is why the loop's action/search levers failed when tried first.

One honest caveat: it's possible the analytic engine is near-optimal for this simulator and the realistic prize is a small-but-clean margin (Wilson LB > 0.5), not domination — set the bar there, as your docs already do. But the reason you've been stuck at parity rather than near it is the three anchors above, and none of them was in the falsified-lever set.
