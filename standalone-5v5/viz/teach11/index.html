The real engine: DP, neural nets, LP & QP in one loop.

The 5-a-side page is the stripped-down cousin — no tabular DP, no formation solver. This is the real thing. Twenty-two players on a full 120×80 pitch, a ~476-number egocentric observation per decision, and a learning loop that actually runs approximate dynamic programming (an EPV value function + DP-cleaned critic targets + backward credit replay), a neural actor/critic over the full field, a Clarabel interior-point linear program for formation, and a quadratic-program model-predictive controller. 01 the state · a ~476-dim partially-observed field

Twenty-two players become ~476 numbers.

The full engine sees far more than the 5-a-side's lean 71. Each decision reads a ~476-dimensional egocentric observation: a self-centred base, a full whole-field motion snapshot of all 22 players and the ball as position, velocity, acceleration and jerk, plus tactical-structure blocks (back-four line, midfield band, spacing) and hidden belief states — Kalman perception confidence, an opponent-press belief, intent. Those beliefs are the "partially observed" part: you act on estimates, not certainty.

Why not a lookup table?

No two of these vectors are ever identical. A table would alias wildly different positions together. That's the whole reason a neural net is needed — to generalize value across states never seen exactly before (§4).

A second, invariant vector for memory

Alongside the policy obs, a 210-number CONFIG vector is canonicalized — orient, mirror, sort — so a shape and its mirror image map to the same 210 numbers. Projected to a 256-dim embedding, it indexes a memory: "have we seen a moment like this before?"

The hidden state

Perception confidence, opponent press, and stamina are never observed directly — the engine carries beliefs over them. That's the defining feature of a POMDP: decide well without full information.

02 the policy · neural net + a DP prior, then sample

The DP prior corrects the myopic pick.

The neural net scores the ~16 legal actions — but it can be short-sighted, preferring the safe pass. So the engine blends in a bounded DP/Q policy prior (weight 0.5) built from replayed outcomes: it knows the through-ball in behind creates more eventual value. blended = neural + 0.5·prior, then a softmax that the policy samples (~70/20/10 over the top options, so it still explores). Sampling, not certainty

The policy samples the blended softmax — mostly the best action, but the 2nd and 3rd a fraction of the time. That controlled exploration (entropy + top-k) is what keeps a neural-authoritative policy learning instead of collapsing onto one safe mode.

03 real dynamic programming · credit replay + EPV value

DP is actually in the loop here.

This is the big upgrade over the 5-a-side. In soccer most good actions aren't rewarded immediately — the ball-winning press matters because it leads, six seconds later, to a goal. So the engine replays each possession backward with the Bellman rule, pushing the delayed outcome onto the decisions that caused it. That's approximate dynamic programming, and it turns a sparse signal into a dense, correctly-credited one the neural net can actually learn from.

05 team shape · a linear program (interior-point)

Formation is an assignment problem.

The 5-a-side deliberately had no formation machinery. The full engine solves a real interior-point linear program every tick (Clarabel, ~550 variables, <5 ms) that picks each player's target position, velocity and acceleration to optimize a weighted tactical objective — "the LP decides the shape, the MPC executes." The clearest way to feel why a global solve beats greedy is its simplest cousin, the role assignment below (a real Hungarian LP, also used inside the rotation planner): send each player to its nearest slot and two of them chase the same spot. Greedy is – worse: two players race to the same slot while a third slot goes unfilled and someone sprints across the pitch to cover. The LP sees the whole board and swaps assignments so the team moves least.

The in-play version is bigger — a Clarabel interior-point solve over pos/vel/accel targets with double-integrator dynamics constraints, to optimality in under 5 ms a tick (a deterministic simplex is available for headless training). It's optimization as team coordination, the counterpart to the per-player QP-MPC that executes each move.

06 the search · optimize 24 knobs, then escape the plateau

Two dozen reward knobs, moving together — and a breakout.

The reward is a set of 24 coupled scale-knobs (goal vs forward-pass vs learned-EPV vs pass-turnover vs giveaways vs shape…). An evolution strategy searches them — but the full engine hits a real plateau: the population settles into a "competent but capped" basin around +2.6. 07 execution · model-predictive control as a real QP

MPC keeps the team's motion feasible — a quadratic program.

Every player wants to sprint straight at the ball — and collide. The engine's per-player MPC (PlanarPointMassMpc, a horizon-12 double-integrator over ~0.8 s) finds the closest feasible motion to that intent: minimize the quadratic ½·Σ‖p−pref‖² + ‖v−vref‖² + ‖a‖² with soft keep-outs around where opponents will be, subject to a circular acceleration limit ‖a‖ ≤ amax. A genuine quadratic program, solved by warm-started projected gradient every tick. (A learned neural-MPC objective head also nudges the target — the same amortization the value critic uses.) The QP objective falls to its minimum

Starting from "everyone straight at the ball" (big overlap penalty), projected steps trade a little intent for separation until no constraint is violated and the deviation is as small as possible — the QP optimum.

In the trainer, a learned world model + shallow neural MCTS score short candidate futures; this QP-MPC then rejects or repairs any that aren't physically feasible. Planning proposes, MPC disposes.

08 co-evolution · self-play league & held-out promotion

Climbing is proven, not claimed.

Self-play can drift into a stalemate, so the frontier trains against a league of frozen past champions and is promoted to new champion only when it beats the current one by a margin (goal-diff ≥ 0.25) — not by raw rank. A separate ELO (base 1500) tracks tournament strength and only ratchets up on promotion. The plateau shows here too — weight-norm inflation stalls the gradient — until the population-escape (§6) breaks it and the ladder climbs again.

09 the whole loop

DP is the bridge; the neural net is the destination.

  • Measure pass/dribble/shot/turnover/conceded-danger outcomes across ticks — reward truth.
  • Approximate DP replays the trajectory and propagates delayed credit backward (§3).
  • Neural actor + critic (MAPPO, 128-wide, set-encoded) generalize the cleaned-up value over the 210-dim field, with a bounded DP/Q prior at decision time (§2, §4).
  • Formation LP assigns roles; world-model + neural MCTS score short futures (§5).
  • QP-MPC makes the chosen motion feasible (§7); promotion is strict held-out vs analytic + protected best (§8).

Population perturbation is only the plateau escape hatch — it widens the basin; gradient RL climbs inside it (§6).

DP vs neural nets — the honest division of labor

Not "DP instead of neural nets." DP improves the learning signal; the neural net generalizes it across continuous soccer. Exact DP can't: soccer has no small finite state table — 22 players, motion, shape, pressure, fatigue, geometry.
